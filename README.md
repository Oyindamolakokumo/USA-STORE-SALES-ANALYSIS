# USA STORE SALES ANALYSIS

### Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive data analysis of a USA-based retail store’s performance, focusing on revenue drivers, operational efficiency, and regional market penetration. By analyzing $73.14M in total revenue, this dashboard identifies key profit centers and highlights critical bottlenecks in the supply chain and pricing strategies.

### Dashboard Access
* **Revenue Analysis:** Focuses on high-level financial KPIs, regional performance, and sales channel effectiveness.
* **Order Analysis:** Deep dives into logistics, discount correlations, and customer-level profitability.

### Problem Statement
Despite achieving a healthy **29% net profit margin**, the business faces three primary challenges:
1.  **Logistics Delays:** An average delivery time of **20.67 days** poses a risk to customer retention.
2.  **Discount Inefficiency:** High discounts are not translating into higher revenue, suggesting a potential loss in brand value or margin leakage.
3.  **Geographic Concentration:** Revenue is heavily skewed toward California, leaving significant untapped potential in other major states like New York.


### Tools & Technologies
* **Data Visualization:** Power BI (as seen in the dashboard captures)
* **Data Processing:** Excel/Power Query (for transformation)
* **Analysis:** Statistical correlation of discounts vs. revenue and trend analysis.

### Key Insights & Findings

#### 1. Financial Health
* **Total Revenue:** $73.14M
* **Total Profit:** $21.33M
* **Avg. Revenue per Order:** ~$9,142 (Indicating a high-end appliance or B2B focus).

#### 2. Regional & Channel Performance
* **The California Goldmine:** California alone generates **$15M**, outperforming Texas ($7M) by over 100%.
* **In-Store Dominance:** Physical stores remain the primary engine, contributing **41.26% ($8.8M)** of total profit.
* **Cost Leaders:** The **Midwest** region carries the highest cost burden at **$15.1M**.

#### 3. Product & Promotion Dynamics
* **Star Product:** Refrigerators are the undisputed leader in the Gifts Promotion category, generating **$67M** in revenue.
* **Discount Paradox:** Revenue peaks at low discount levels (5%–8%). Deep discounts (>20%) result in a sharp revenue decline, proving that customers are driven by value rather than price-slashing.

#### 4. Operational Metrics
* **Delivery Lag:** The average delivery window of **20.67 days** is the most significant operational weakness identified.

### Strategic Recommendations
* **Logistics Overhaul:** Prioritize a review of the "Last Mile" delivery process to reduce the 20-day lag. Shorter delivery times will likely increase the lifetime value of the 50 key high-value customers.
* **Discount Optimization:** Phase out deep discounting strategies. Focus marketing efforts on "Value-Add" promotions for top-tier products like Refrigerators.
* **Regional Expansion:** Replicate the California sales model in New York and Florida. Conduct a gap analysis to see why New York revenue ($4M) lags so far behind California.
* **Customer Retention:** Develop a loyalty program specifically for top-tier accounts like **Medline** ($0.62M profit contribution) to protect the most profitable revenue streams.
